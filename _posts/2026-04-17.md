I had always wanted to attend a conference. All the talks I had given were local, but I wanted to experience what it felt like to step outside my comfort zone and be in a room full of experts discussing work related to my own research.

Within the span of two months, I attended two conferences: CRAWD and the North Central Avian Disease Conference. I’m deeply grateful to my advisor for the encouragement and support that made this possible.

Now I want to highlight what I learned from attending these conferences.

## Need for Data Scientists

There is a growing need for data scientists in fields where large datasets are collected. The ability to work with big data, interpret it, and help stakeholders make informed decisions is incredibly valuable.

However, one challenge is that the right data is not always available. In some cases, there is significant missingness, or the data is not collected in a useful format. For example, veterinarians may not always record the necessary metadata, or they may do so inconsistently. A talk on predictive modeling in avian disease highlighted how this issue affects the industry.

I also realized that even in conferences that are not strictly technical, there are many technically skilled people present. This creates great opportunities for networking, feedback, and improving your work.

## How to Present

A friend once asked me if I get nervous before giving a talk. The truth is—everyone does. I’ve learned to accept that nervousness as part of the process rather than something to fight.

What matters more is my mindset. I remind myself that I’ve prepared, that I’m excited to share my work, and that this is an opportunity to learn from the audience as well. Any feedback I receive is a bonus. Simply sharing the work is already a win.

## Alone vs. Together

For the first conference, I attended alone, which pushed me to actively introduce myself and meet new people. While that experience helped me grow, I also really enjoyed attending with my team at the second conference.

Being there together created more opportunities to bond, build trust, and support each other. That sense of connection can carry over into future collaborations and projects.

## Business Meeting

For the first time, I attended a business meeting at a conference. It gave me insight into what happens behind the scenes—how conferences are organized, planned, and managed.

As an attendee, you also get to vote and contribute to decisions, which shifts your role from just a participant to a stakeholder. Surprisingly, this ended up being one of the most enjoyable parts of the experience.

## Final Thoughts

Overall, I had an amazing time presenting my work, communicating with others, and building new connections. These conferences pushed me out of my comfort zone in the best way, and I’m looking forward to attending many more in the future.
